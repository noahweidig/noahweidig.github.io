abstract: >-
  Often considered more a problem for Western North America, wildfires are
  becoming more intense, frequent and damaging in the East, such as this week’s
  blaze that destroyed dozens of homes in Georgia, fire scientists said.
  Researchers blame a number of factors including climate change causing fuel to
  dry out and be more flammable, a record drought, tens of millions of tons of
  dead trees from Hurricane Helene and just the large area where dense forests
  and high numbers of people try to coexist.
